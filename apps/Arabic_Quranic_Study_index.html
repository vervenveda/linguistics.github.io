Week 1 · Meeting the Qur'an

Orientation, structure, adab (etiquette) and four short sūrahs.
Track: Beginner
Format: 6 weeks · self-paced

Week 1 · Meeting the Qur'an

This week builds a gentle foundation: what the Qur'an is, how it is structured, and how to approach it with intention and respect. You get to know Sūrat al-Fātiḥah and three short sūrahs of protection.

Suggested pace: 4–5 short sessions, 30–40 minutes each.
Session 1: What is the Qur'an?
Big picture · structure · purpose
  • Learn key terms: Qur'an, sūrah, āyah, tilāwah, tadabbur.
  • Understand basic structure: chapters, verses, Makki/Madani.
  • Journal your intention for this 6-week journey.
Session 2: Al-Fātiḥah – The Opening
Heart of prayer
  • Read al-Fātiḥah with a translation you trust.
  • Rewrite each line's meaning in your own words.
  • Begin memorizing or refining your recitation.
Session 3: Short Sūrahs of Protection
Al-Ikhlāṣ · Al-Falaq · An-Nās
  • Read the three short sūrahs and their translations.
  • Note what each sūrah protects you from.
  • Choose one to recite before sleep this week.
Session 4: Listening Practice
Sound and heart
  • Listen to a reciter of al-Fātiḥah and the three short sūrahs.
  • Follow along visually as you listen.
  • Journal how listening feels different from reading silently.
Tip: keep a small “Qur'an journal” nearby and write freely. No one else ever has to read it.

Week 2 · Light, Mercy & Remembrance

This week focuses on verses about Allah's light, mercy and closeness to the heart that remembers Him. You begin linking spontaneous remembrance (dhikr) to what you read.

Core passages include: 24:35, 13:28, 2:255, 14:7 and signs in creation.
Session 1: Light (Nūr)
“Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth…”
  • Read a translation of 24:35 (at least the beginning).
  • Reflect: where do you most need light on your path right now?
  • Write a one-line du'a asking for light in that area.
Session 2: Tranquility of the Heart
Remembrance and rest
  • Read 13:28 and 2:152–153.
  • Choose one short dhikr phrase to repeat gently for 3–5 minutes.
  • Journal how your body and breath feel afterwards.
Session 3: Ayat al-Kursi
2:255 · protection & majesty
  • Read Ayat al-Kursi slowly, phrase by phrase.
  • Underline each description of Allah.
  • Note which description brings you the most comfort.
Session 4: Gratitude & Creation
Blessings and signs
  • Read 14:7 and a few verses about creation (for example 55:1–13).
  • Make a list of ten blessings you can see today.
  • Connect each blessing back to its Giver in your journal.
Try pairing verses with simple phrases: light → hope, remembrance → calm, gratitude → expansion.

Week 3 · Stories of the Prophets

This week you walk with Yūsuf, Maryam, Ayyūb and others. You learn how the Qur'an uses story as a school of patience, trust and resilience.

Core passages: Sūrah Yūsuf 12, Sūrah Maryam 19, Sūrah al-Anbiyā 21:83–90.
Session 1: Why Stories?
Lessons inside narrative
  • Read the conclusion of Sūrah Yūsuf (12:111) in translation.
  • Journal: what do you usually look for in stories – comfort, answers, courage?
  • Write one sentence about what you hope to learn from prophetic stories.
Session 2: Yūsuf – Beautiful Patience
From the dream to the well
  • Read an early portion of Sūrah Yūsuf (for example 12:4–20).
  • List each moment of unfairness or hardship Yūsuf faces.
  • Reflect on “beautiful patience” and where you may need it.
Session 3: Maryam – Strength in Solitude
Maryam in the palm grove
  • Read 19:16–26 about Maryam's trial.
  • Notice all the ways Allah supports her in isolation.
  • Write a note to yourself for a time you might feel alone.
Session 4: Ayyūb & Other Prophets
Calling on Allah
  • Read 21:83–90 and focus on each prophet's du'a.
  • Choose one short prophetic supplication to keep this week.
  • Repeat it during a quiet moment each day.
Remember: these stories are not distant legends – they are mirrors for your own heart and choices.

Week 4 · Worship, Prayer & Heart Work

Worship in the Qur'an is not cold ritual; it is a living relationship. This week connects prayer, fasting and du'a to the inner work of the heart.

Core passages: Al-Fātiḥah again, 2:183–186, 29:45, 73:1–10.
Session 1: Why We Worship
Al-Fātiḥah revisited
  • Return to Sūrat al-Fātiḥah with fresh eyes.
  • Focus on “You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help”.
  • Journal how that line could reshape one ordinary day.
Session 2: Fasting & Awareness
2:183–185
  • Read verses about fasting and their purpose.
  • Note the link between fasting and becoming more mindful of Allah.
  • Think of one small habit you could “fast” from for the sake of your heart.
Session 3: Du'a – Allah is Near
Calling and being answered
  • Read 2:186 about Allah's nearness to those who call on Him.
  • Create a simple du'a map (family, health, faith, work, healing).
  • Write one sincere du'a in each circle.
Session 4: Prayer & Night Quiet
29:45 and 73:1–10
  • Read about prayer holding you back from what harms you.
  • Read about gentle night worship in Sūrat al-Muzzammil.
  • Try spending a few extra quiet minutes at night with Qur'an or du'a.
Worship is how the heart learns to turn – not just a list of duties.

Week 5 · Character, Community & Everyday Life

This week explores verses that shape character and community: how we speak, how we treat parents, and how we stand with the vulnerable.

Core passages: 49:10–13, 17:23–39, 31:12–19.
Session 1: Human Dignity
Surah Al-Hujurāt
  • Read verses about backbiting, mockery and the honor of every people.
  • Reflect on places where harmful speech appears in your world.
  • Choose one speech habit to improve this week.
Session 2: Parents & Family
Kindness and humility
  • Read the verses about honoring parents in Sūrat al-Isrā (17:23–24).
  • Write a du'a for your parents or those who raised you.
  • Plan one concrete act of kindness you can offer them.
Session 3: Balance & Generosity
Spending, waste and greed
  • Read verses about giving, wasting and miserliness.
  • Reflect on your relationship with money and things.
  • Decide on one small act of secret charity this week.
Session 4: Luqmān’s Advice
Inner and outer manners
  • Read Luqmān's advice to his son in Sūrat Luqmān.
  • Note guidance about gratitude, prayer, humility and voice.
  • Choose one piece of advice as your “theme sentence” for the week.
Character is Qur'an walking in daily life: on the tongue, in the home, at work and online.

Week 6 · Living with the Qur'an Daily

The final week is about building a sustainable, gentle daily routine: short sūrahs that live with you, and a simple plan you can keep.

Core passages: Sūrat al-'Asr, ash-Shams, al-Layl, ad-Duḥā, ash-Sharḥ.
Session 1: The Weight of Time
Sūrat al-'Asr
  • Read Sūrat al-'Asr and reflect on time, loss and salvation.
  • Write where most of your time currently goes.
  • Note one shift you can make toward what truly matters.
Session 2: Light After Difficulty
Sūrat ad-Duḥā and ash-Sharḥ
  • Read the sūrahs revealed after a period of heaviness.
  • Remember times Allah brought you through hardship.
  • Write a few lines as if these sūrahs were speaking to your past self.
Session 3: Inner Struggle
Sūrat ash-Shams and al-Layl
  • Read about the soul, purification and corruption.
  • Identify one habit you want to purify.
  • Pair that habit with a small Qur'an-related practice this week.
Session 4: Build Your Ongoing Routine
Your personal plan
  • Design a daily Qur'an practice you can actually keep.
  • Include reading or listening, reflection and du'a.
  • Decide on your minimum for hard days and your ideal for good days.
The goal is not to finish the Qur'an quickly, but to keep walking with it slowly for a lifetime.
