Week 1 · Arabic Text & Recitation Craft

Deepening your relationship with the Arabic script, tajwīd basics and how you approach the mushaf itself.
Level: Advanced beginner / intermediate
Format: 6 weeks · self-guided

Week 1 · Arabic Text & Recitation Craft

This week you move from simply “seeing Arabic letters” to treating the Qur'anic script as a living map: letters, vowels, tajwīd symbols and the etiquette of handling the mushaf with intention.

Focus: tajwīd vocabulary, makhārij awareness, and building a recitation routine.
Session 1: Script as Sacred Geometry
Alif to yā · dots · shapes
  • Review all 28 letters, paying attention to where each is written and spoken.
  • Circle any letters you still mix up (for example ث / س / ص).
  • Write a short note on how it feels to see Allah's words in their original form.
Session 2: Tajwīd Map – Key Terms
Makhārij · ṣifāt · ghunnah · qalqalah
  • Create a tiny glossary for 5–7 tajwīd terms with simple meanings.
  • Listen to a reciter and try to “spot” these features in a short sūrah.
  • Highlight one term you want to focus on this week.
Session 3: Your Recitation Baseline
Gentle self-audit
  • Record yourself reciting al-Fātiḥah and one short sūrah.
  • Listen back once with kindness, noting strength and growth areas.
  • Write down 2 small improvements to work on (for example elongation, clarity).
Session 4: Mushaf Etiquette
Adab with the Book
  • Review etiquette: cleanliness, intention, posture, distractions.
  • Design a “Qur'an corner” or small space that feels dignified and calm.
  • Commit to a fixed recitation time for this 6-week workshop.
Advanced does not mean harsh: your recitation journey is a craft polished over years, not a performance graded in days.

Week 2 · Thematic Reading & Mapping

This week you learn to read across sūrahs through themes: mercy, justice, patience, gratitude, light, and more – building your own “theme maps” in your journal.

Focus: connecting scattered verses into living themes in your life.
Session 1: Choose Your Theme
Mercy · justice · patience · gratitude · tawakkul
  • Pick one theme that you need most this season.
  • List 3–5 verses you already know or have heard related to that theme.
  • Write why this theme calls to you now.
Session 2: Verse Clusters
Collecting ayaat
  • Use a Quran index or app to find 3–5 new verses linked to your theme.
  • Copy key phrases for each verse into your journal.
  • Note which surahs and contexts they appear in.
Session 3: Heart Map
From page to pattern
  • Draw a simple mind map: theme in the middle, verses as branches.
  • Next to each verse, add one line: “This speaks to…” + a part of your life.
  • Circle verses that feel like direct personal guidance.
Session 4: Walking the Theme
Embodied Qur'an
  • Choose one verse as your “daily companion” for a week.
  • Recite it at least twice a day (morning and evening).
  • Write down one moment when it changed your reaction or choice.
Over time, you will build a personal atlas of Qur'anic themes – a map of verses that have walked with you.

Week 3 · Makki · Madani & Context of Revelation

This week deepens your sense of when verses arrived in history: Makki and Madani surahs, context of revelation (asbāb al-nuzūl) and how seerah and Quran mirror each other.

Focus: reading verses with their historical sky still around them.
Session 1: Makki vs. Madani Signatures
Early vs. later revelation
  • List typical features of Makki surahs (for example short, powerful, core beliefs).
  • List typical features of Madani surahs (for example law, community, detail).
  • Pick one surah and identify which “signature” it matches.
Session 2: Asbāb al-Nuzūl (Reasons of Revelation)
Verses with stories
  • Choose 2–3 well-known verses with known historical contexts.
  • Write their background in a few simple sentences.
  • Reflect on how context deepens the verse's meaning for you today.
Session 3: Seerah & Surah
Biography + Revelation
  • Pick one seerah moment (for example, Hijrah, Badr, Ta'if).
  • Identify which surahs or verses connect to that moment.
  • Write how knowing the story changes how you hear the verses.
Session 4: Mushaf Order vs. Chronology
Why this sequence?
  • Read about how the current mushaf order differs from chronological order.
  • Choose one interesting example of a surah's place in the mushaf.
  • Journal: what might be the wisdom of this ordering for a reader's heart?
Context is like the sky behind the stars: the stars do not disappear without it, but they shine differently when you see where they hang.

Week 4 · Inner States & Tazkiyah

This week turns strongly inward: the Qur'anic language of the self (nafs), the heart (qalb), sincerity (ikhlāṣ) and hidden diseases like showiness (riyā) or arrogance.

Focus: verses that diagnose and heal the inner world.
Session 1: Three Faces of the Nafs
Ammārah · Lawwāmah · Muṭma'innah
  • Study verses that mention these states of the soul.
  • Write a simple description and everyday example of each.
  • Identify where you most often find yourself lately.
Session 2: Heart Diseases in the Qur'an
Hardness · hypocrisy · arrogance
  • Collect 3–4 verses where “disease in the heart” or hardness is mentioned.
  • Note the behaviors or attitudes that accompany these descriptions.
  • Journal one small sign you want to watch for in yourself without shame.
Session 3: Sincerity & Hidden Acts
Ikhlāṣ vs. riyā
  • Read verses about doing deeds “for Allah alone”.
  • Plan one recurring act of worship that only you and Allah know.
  • Write how it feels to hold something purely for that private bond.
Session 4: Qur'anic Du'as for Tazkiyah
Supplications of purification
  • Choose 2–3 Quranic supplications about forgiveness, guidance, or purification.
  • Memorize at least one in Arabic or in your language.
  • Set a daily time to repeat it slowly with full attention.
Tazkiyah is not self-hatred – it is Allah-led gardening of the soul: pruning, watering, and trusting the light.

Week 5 · Justice, Care & Society

This week widens your gaze: from personal heart work to Qur'anic calls for justice, care for the vulnerable, fair trade, and stewardship of the earth.

Focus: Qur'an as a charter for ethical society, not just private devotion.
Session 1: Scales, Contracts & Trusts
Weights, measures, promises
  • Read verses about honest weights and measures, fulfilling trusts and contracts.
  • Note where these issues appear today (business, online work, promises).
  • Choose one practical change in how you handle commitments.
Session 2: Orphans, Poor & Marginalized
Who the Qur'an defends
  • Collect verses about orphans, the poor and those without protection.
  • Write how your community includes or overlooks these groups.
  • Plan one small act of support you can realistically offer.
Session 3: Speaking for Justice
Standing even against ourselves
  • Read verses that command standing firmly for justice.
  • Think of one situation where speaking up felt hard.
  • Journal what “Qur'anic courage” would have looked like in that moment.
Session 4: Earth as Amānah
Creation as trust
  • Read verses describing the earth, rain, animals and balance.
  • Reflect on your lifestyle's impact on that balance.
  • Choose one concrete stewardship action (for example waste, water, kindness to creatures).
The Qur'an does not only ask “Who are you becoming?” but also “What kind of world are you helping to build?”.

Week 6 · Lifelong Qur'an Projects

The final week is about designing your personal “Qur'an project”: memorization goals, Arabic study, teaching, service, or a blend of all, at a pace that honors your life.

Focus: realistic planning, gentle ambition and long-term companionship.
Session 1: Your Qur'an Story So Far
Seeing the path behind you
  • Write a one-page reflection on your history with the Qur'an.
  • Highlight three turning points: people, moments, or verses.
  • Thank Allah for each turning point in your own words.
Session 2: Choosing a Primary Project
Hifdh · Arabic · teaching · service
  • List all the directions that attract you (memorizing, studying Arabic, teaching kids, etc.).
  • Rank them by: “most needed for my heart” and “most realistic right now”.
  • Pick one main project for the next 6–12 months.
Session 3: Designing a Gentle Plan
Minimum & ideal days
  • Create two versions of your routine: “hard day minimum” and “good day ideal”.
  • Include time for recitation, understanding and reflection.
  • Write how you will reset kindly when you fall behind.
Session 4: Accountability & Du'a
Not walking alone
  • Decide who or what will gently hold you accountable (friend, teacher, journal, app).
  • Write a “Qur'an covenant du'a” asking Allah to keep you close to His book.
  • Choose a date 6 months from now to re-read this covenant.
Advanced does not mean rushing ahead of life – it means weaving the Qur'an into who you are becoming, year after year.
